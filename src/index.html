Girls Code Lincoln

formerly Lincoln Girls Who Code

Teaching girls to code through creativity, bravery, and teamwork.

find out more!
Girls Who Code is a national nonprofit organization, dedicated to closing the technology gender gap. They've done this by creating a brand and a curriculum that can be implemented by anyone across the country. The curriculum has four main principles: teaching the "Core 4" (variables, loops, functions, conditionals), showing girls real-life women who do amazing things in tech, giving time to share experiences as girls in the world, and doing something with all this via the CS Impact Project. Find out more info about them here.

Lincoln Girls Who Code began in January 2016 with the inspired gusto of Lana Zumbrunn (formerly at Fuse) and Jessica Versaw (Hudl). Since that time, dozens of girls across multiple clubs sites have had the opportunity to be exposed to coding and interact with leaders from the tech community. The girls have had opportunities to help plan and build community impact projects, and have presented at local tech meetups.

Lincoln Girls Who Code is run by a small group of advocates from across the tech community who meet at least once a month to strategize and ensure the ongoing success of the organization. There are several opportunities to get involved with Lincoln Girls Who Code, which you can check out here. Whether you’d just like to help facilitate one session of the club, or would like to have an ongoing role in the success of the organization, we’d love to hear from you.

If you are interested in finding out more, getting a Girls Who Code club happening somewhere near you, or getting involved, here are some great resources for you to utilize...

  • attend one of our monthly meetings
  • fill out our volunteer form

FAQ

What is the application process?

Fill out the application by the date/time specified, share your heart and mind with us about who you are, get excited and ready for coding, attend the pre-launch info session, and let us know any questions!

Do I need a laptop?

No! We have some generously provided by Hudl. If you have a machine that you prefer working on, please let one of the instructors know.

Are snacks provided?

Yes!!!! Generously, thanks to the wonderful folks at Hudl.

Do I need experience with coding?

Absolutely not! We just hope you will be willing to be brave with us, and loving with everyone involved.

What if I fall outside the age range?

Sorry! Maybe next year! We hope to start more clubs that accomodate more ages, but at this point we do not have the capacity. Regardless of your experience, we want you to wait until you fall in that range.

Does it cost money?

No!! All the teachers are volunteers, who just love and care about this, and we have received some generous support from Hudl to help us get this ball rolling!

I have so many more questions!! How do I get in touch?

Email us at girlswhocodelincoln@gmail.com

I want to get involved. What do I do?

Fill out the volunteer application and tell us about yourself!! If you are really serious, come to one of our monthly planning meetings! (check out the calendar). Feel free to email us.

I want you to start a Girls Who Code club at my school.

Wow, we think that is awesome! While we don’t currently have the bandwidth to organize another club with our current pool of volunteers, we’d be happy to chat with you about our process, what we’ve learned, and point you in the direction to get started.
